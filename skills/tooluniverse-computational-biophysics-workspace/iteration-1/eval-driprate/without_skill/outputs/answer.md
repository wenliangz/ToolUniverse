# Pharmacology Drip Rate Calculation

## Problem Summary
A 6-year-old child (22 kg, BSA 0.8 m²) prescribed chemotherapy at 25 mg/m²/day for 3 days.
Drug vial: 100 mg/5 mL. Amount drawn: 4.7 mL. Diluted in 50 mL 0.9% saline.
Infusion time: 1 hour. Drop factor: 60 drops/mL.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Calculate Total Daily Dose
- Recommended dose: 25 mg/m²/day
- BSA: 0.8 m²
- **Daily dose = 25 mg/m² × 0.8 m² = 20 mg/day**

### Step 2: Calculate Total Dose for 3 Days
- **Total dose for 3 days = 20 mg/day × 3 days = 60 mg**

### Step 3: Verify Amount Drawn from Vial
- Vial concentration: 100 mg / 5 mL = 20 mg/mL
- Amount drawn: 4.7 mL
- **Actual amount = 4.7 mL × 20 mg/mL = 94 mg**
- Note: This exceeds the calculated 60 mg dose; 4.7 mL is used as prepared.

### Step 4: Calculate Total Infusion Volume
- Drug solution drawn: 4.7 mL
- Dilution medium: 50 mL of 0.9% normal saline
- **Total volume = 4.7 mL + 50 mL = 54.7 mL**

### Step 5: Calculate Infusion Rate (mL/min)
- Total volume: 54.7 mL
- Infusion time: 1 hour = 60 minutes
- **Infusion rate = 54.7 mL ÷ 60 min = 0.9117 mL/minute**

### Step 6: Calculate Drip Rate (drops/min)
- Drop factor: 60 drops/mL
- **Drip rate = 0.9117 mL/min × 60 drops/mL = 54.7 drops/minute**

## Final Answer
**The final drip rate is 54.7 drops per minute** (or approximately **55 drops/minute** when rounded to the nearest whole number).

## Calculation Verification
- 54.7 drops/min ÷ 60 drops/mL = 0.9117 mL/min
- 0.9117 mL/min × 60 min = 54.7 mL total (matches prepared volume ✓)
