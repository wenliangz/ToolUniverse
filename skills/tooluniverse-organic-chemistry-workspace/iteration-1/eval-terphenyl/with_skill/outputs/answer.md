# IUPAC Name of the Grignard Substitution Product

## Question
What is the IUPAC name of the product of 1,3-dibromo-2-iodobenzene and excess phenyl magnesium bromide refluxing in THF after aqueous work-up?

## Reasoning

### Step 1: Identify Reactive Sites
- **Starting material:** 1,3-dibromo-2-iodobenzene has three halogen substituents on a benzene ring
- **Reagent:** PhMgBr (phenyl magnesium bromide) is a strong nucleophile (phenyl anion equivalent)
- **Reaction type:** Ionic substitution on aromatic halides

### Step 2: Halogen Reactivity Hierarchy
Halogens react with Grignard reagents in order of leaving group ability:
- **I > Br > Cl**
- Iodine has the weakest C-I bond and is the best leaving group
- Bromine is a moderate leaving group

### Step 3: Reaction with Excess Grignard Reagent
Since we have **excess PhMgBr**:

1. **First substitution (position 2):** The C-I bond is attacked; I⁻ leaves
   - 2-I → 2-Ph (iodine replaced by phenyl)

2. **Second and third substitutions (positions 1 and 3):** With excess reagent, both C-Br bonds are attacked
   - 1-Br → 1-Ph (bromine replaced by phenyl)
   - 3-Br → 3-Ph (bromine replaced by phenyl)

### Step 4: Product After Aqueous Work-up
After aqueous work-up (quenches excess Grignard), all three halogens have been replaced by phenyl groups:
- Central benzene ring with three phenyl substituents at positions 1, 2, and 3

### Step 5: Verification
- **Molecular formula:** C₂₄H₁₈ ✓
- **Degrees of unsaturation:** (2×24 + 2 - 18) / 2 = 16 (four aromatic rings) ✓
- **Valence:** All atoms satisfied ✓

## Final Answer

**1,2,3-triphenylbenzene**

Alternatively known as:
- ortho-terphenyl with an additional phenyl substituent
- 1,2,3-triphenylarene (less common)

The standard IUPAC name is **1,2,3-triphenylbenzene**.
