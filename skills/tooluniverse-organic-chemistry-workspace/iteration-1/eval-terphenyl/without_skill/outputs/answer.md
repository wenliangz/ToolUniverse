# Grignard Reaction Product Identification

## Starting Material
1,3-dibromo-2-iodobenzene: A benzene ring with:
- Bromine at position 1
- Iodine at position 2
- Bromine at position 3

## Reaction Mechanism
**Reagent**: Excess phenyl magnesium bromide (C₆H₅-MgBr)
**Conditions**: Reflux in THF, then aqueous work-up

## Reasoning

### Step 1: Grignard Reactivity
Grignard reagents are strong nucleophiles that displace halides from aromatic rings through nucleophilic aromatic substitution. The reactivity order for leaving group ability is: I > Br > Cl

### Step 2: Substitution Pattern
With excess phenyl Grignard reagent, all three halides are displaced:
- Iodine at position 2 (most reactive leaving group)
- Bromines at positions 1 and 3

Each halogen is replaced by a phenyl group (C₆H₅-) from the Grignard reagent.

### Step 3: Product Structure
Starting material: C₆H₃(Br)₂(I) - benzene with 3 halogen substituents
Product: C₆H₃(C₆H₅)₃ - benzene with 3 phenyl substituents at positions 1, 2, and 3

### Step 4: IUPAC Nomenclature
The product is a benzene ring substituted with three phenyl groups at positions 1, 2, and 3.

## Final Answer

**1,2,3-triphenylbenzene**

Alternative IUPAC names:
- 1,2,3-tri(phenyl)benzene
- 1-(phenylmethyl)-2,3-diphenylbenzene (less common)

The most systematic IUPAC name is **1,2,3-triphenylbenzene**.
